The First 10 Minutes After You Wake Up

By MindShift Daily Team · Updated July 2026

Most people lose their morning before they even get out of bed. The phone comes on, the scrolling starts, and within ninety seconds the brain is already reacting to other people's news, other people's opinions, and other people's demands. By the time you stand up, your morning is no longer yours — it belongs to whatever app opened first.

The good news is that this is completely reversible, and it doesn't require a dramatic 5am routine. It just requires deciding, in advance, what the first ten minutes will contain.

01Light before screens

Natural light is the single strongest signal you can give your body clock. Even two or three minutes near a window, before any screen, tells your brain that the day has genuinely started. People who do this consistently report falling asleep more easily at night too — the two are more connected than they seem.

02One decision, made the night before

Willpower is at its weakest right after waking up. So the trick is to remove the need for a decision entirely. Lay out your clothes, write the first task on a sticky note, or set out the glass for water — whatever the one thing is, decide it the night before so your groggy morning brain doesn't have to.

03Move before you check anything

It doesn't need to be a workout. Standing up, stretching for sixty seconds, or walking to another room before opening any app breaks the trance of the bed and gives your body a small, early win. Small wins early in the day tend to compound — people who move first thing report feeling more in control of the hours that follow.

None of this requires more time than scrolling would have taken. It just requires deciding, on purpose, what fills those first ten minutes — before anything else gets the chance to.
